Pollutant Deep Dive

Understand what you're breathing.

PM2.5 are tiny particles in the air, 2.5 micrometers or less in size (about 30 times smaller than a human hair). They come from combustion (vehicles, power plants, fires) and can get deep into your lungs and bloodstream, causing serious health problems.

PM10 are particles 10 micrometers or smaller. They include dust, pollen, and mold. They are less dangerous than PM2.5 but can still irritate your eyes, nose, and throat, and aggravate conditions like asthma.

Ground-level ozone (O3) is not emitted directly but forms when sunlight reacts with pollutants from cars and industrial plants. It's the main component of smog. Inhaling ozone can reduce lung function and cause shortness of breath, coughing, and chest pain.

Carbon Monoxide (CO) is an odorless, colorless gas formed by incomplete combustion of fuel (cars, furnaces, stoves). It's dangerous because it reduces the amount of oxygen that can be transported in the bloodstream to critical organs like the heart and brain.
